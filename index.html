💡 Database Guide — When to Use Which

🎓 Google Scholar
Best for: broad cross-disciplinary search, citations, finding an author’s full profile. Covers books, theses, grey literature.
🔮 OpenAlex
Best for: open data, citation graphs, filtering by discipline/institution/year. Fully free API. 250M+ works.
🧠 Semantic Scholar
Best for: AI-generated summaries, TLDR, influence scores, finding semantically similar papers.
🏥 PubMed
Best for: biomedical & life sciences. MeSH controlled vocabulary. Clinical trials, systematic reviews. NIH-curated.
📈 arXiv
Best for: cutting-edge CS, physics, math, economics preprints. Papers appear days after submission — fastest source.
💻 DBLP
Best for: CS conference & journal papers. Find all papers by a CS author, disambiguated. Clean and fast.
📚 JSTOR
Best for: humanities, social science, economics — especially older journal archives. Deep backfile coverage.
🌎 Web of Science
Best for: citation analysis, journal impact factors, finding highly cited works. Institutional access usually required.
